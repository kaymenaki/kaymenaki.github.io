Joint Oceanic Nations

The main inspiration for my project is living underwater, in a world where the four seasons no longer exist. One of the aims of the currency I am designing will be to retain the memory and feeling of the four seasons through an adaptive user interface.

In particular, in this project, I am designing the currency of the Oceanic Nations, banknote cards and c-mist travel cards. Strongly inspired by the Norwegian currency, the majority of the design elements are abstract and shape-based vectors. As mentioned above, my concept in this project is to create banknotes and cards with adaptive digital interfaces that change based on the season.

Logo

The main idea of the logo is a blue triangle, which reflects the feeling of progress and development in an underwater world. The complete logo is an abstract shape, that reminds a windmill vector. It comes as a result of multiplying the main triangle ten times, which represents the ten united nations. The windmill also represents the need for renewable energy sources in the underwater world.

Currency

The currency is the common Dollar which is symbolised by a D with a strike through it. I have chosen a clean and simple font for the currency symbol, in order to make it easier to be hand-written. The top left line is slightly extended to the left, to give the feeling of stability and harmony. This has been inspired by a similar technique used in the symbol of the Euro currency.

Season symbols

Symbols will be used for the adaptive interfaces and each one represent the seasons of the year. I have used a raindrop for autumn, snowflake for winter, flower for spring and sun for summer.

Bank note

On the front of the one-hundred-dollar banknotes, I am using an abstract triangle shape which contains smaller triangles. Also, essential elements, such as the National Bank Director’s signature, the note’s ordinal number and the name of the Nation, are displayed on this side. In addition, there is a symbol on the left-hand side of the note, which reflects the current season of the year by displaying a relevant symbol. I have designed four original vector symbols, one for each season (raindrop for autumn, snowflake for winter, flower for spring and sun for summer). Also, the theme colour changes based on the season.

For the back side of the banknote, I have designed a wavy vector abstract of curved lines, which reflects underwater life and the water’s constant movement. Also, a globe - for the 100 cred note, or a dome - for the 1000 cred note, emerges from these lines which represents living under and over the sea surface. The adaptive symbol is on the top left corner and the globe/dome are displayed on the bottom right corner as watermarks. Also, the value of the note is displayed twice on each side.

Bank card

For the banknote card, I have used a similar triangle abstract shape for the front side but also, the value is displayed on a top layer of the abstract. On the back side, I have chosen a more futuristic interface. A dark blue gradient has been used for the background and submarine instruments are decorating this side of the card and the adaptive symbol is located on the centre of the card.

The most important feature of the banknote card is the functionality of the two fingerprint icons. To reduce the risk of theft and to render cards unusable if accidentally lost, a technology has been attached to it, which requires fingerprint registration in order to use it. On the bottom right corner there is a fingerprint icon and on the top-left there is a slightly larger fingerprint with a loading bar below it. During a transaction, the owner of the card and the receiver must place their fingers on each icon at the same time for few seconds. Once the progress bar is fully loaded, it flashes green which indicates that the transaction is completed, and the card now is registered to the new person. This function does not permit a card to be used by anyone other than its legal owner.

Coins

Coins will be round-shaped and made by glass and aluminium. On the front side, there is a small wavy abstract with the value of the coin, the name of the union and the ten triangles logo as background. On the back side, there is the year of the union’s establishment, a wavy abstract pattern representing the sea and a vector shape of a half-sunk globe for the 10-cred coin logo and a vector shape of a dome for the 5-cred coin. The coins will have a gold/silver colour theme during daytime and will turn into a dark fluo theme colour at night.

C-mist card

Curved lines are also used on the C-Mist card. On both sides, the main concept is life below the surface and how people can take advantage of the high level of technology to communicate and survive underwater. This is reflected by the human-shaped vectors that seem to emerge from the sea on a globe background. In addition, there is a small bar that reflects the credit balance based on the average weekly spend.


On the front side, both the logo and the name of the union are essential assets, as well as a small button with an arrow, which expands into a small screen and you can find the best route for your destination and the relevant travel icon is highlighted each time. Also, a tourist attraction is displayed on the background based on the location of the user, and this will be the adaptive element of the travel card. Click on the left/right arrows and toggle between the available attractions.

Propaganda poster

For the propaganda poster, I am promoting Edinburgh as a heritage city destination. The poster shows a recognisable Edinburgh attraction point in four different windows, one for each season. In this future world, people will be able to visit VR auditoriums and live virtual experiences. This poster will advertise an outdoors entertainment experience of the main attractions of Edinburgh in different seasons of the year.
